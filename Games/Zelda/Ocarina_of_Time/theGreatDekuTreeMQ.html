Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time

Walkthrough

The Deku Tree

The The Great Deku Tree congratulates you for the demonstrated courage for carrying out his wishes. The The Great Deku Tree has more to tell Link. The The Great Deku Tree was cursed by a man of the desert. The evil man uses his vile, sorcerous powers in his search for the Sacred Realm that is connected to Hyrule. The Sacred Realm holds the sacred Triforce that contains the essence of the gods.

The The Great Deku Tree begins to explan how the Goddesses form the land of Hyrule. Three Golden Goddesses descended upon the land of Hyrule. Din, the goddess of power, Nayru, the goddess of wisdom and Farore, the goddess of courage. Din cultivated the land and created the red earth. Nayru poured wisdom onto the earth and gave the spirit of law to the world. Farore, produced all life forms who would uphold the law. Once completed they departed for the heavens. At the point where the goddesses left the world the sacred Triforce remained. The resting place of the Triforce has become the Sacred Realm.

The The Great Deku Tree has tasked Link to never allow the desert man in black armor to lay his hands on the Sacred Triforce. Link was able to break the curse, but the The Great Deku Tree was doomed before he started and will pass soon. Link is Hyrule's final hope, now go to Hyrule Castle and meet the Princess of Destiny. The The Great Deku Tree gives Link the (Kokiri's Emerald), the stone the man wanted so much, that he cast the curse on the The Great Deku Tree. The The Great Deku Tree gives his farewell goodbye then passes. Navi instructs Link to go to Hyrule Castle.

Return to the Kokiri Forest and meet up with Mido blocking your path. He is not happy to hear about The Great Deku Tree passing, he will then let you pass. Now is the time to leave the Kokiri Forest. Head all the way to the west side of the area to leave. The kid blocking the path will now allow you to enter. As you start to run across the bridge Link hears Saria's voice. She knew that Link would leave the forest someday becasue he is different from Saria and her friends. But that's ok, because we'll be friends forever. She gives Link the (Fairy Ocarina) as a show of them being true friends. When you play my (Fairy Ocarina), I hope you will think of me and come back to the forest to visi. Link then runs off.
